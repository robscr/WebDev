HOW TO PLAY CHESSLESHIP!

AIM

The aim of Chessleship is to uncover all the hidden pieces with the least amount of squares guessed.

Guesses and Turns

You have a maximum of 8 turns to reveal all the pieces, and want to use as few guesses as possible across those turns.

Each turn, you can guess up to 8 squares by selecting them with mouse/touch. The less squares you guess, the better your score will be. You can deselect a selected square by pressing it again.
To end your turn, press the 'Guess' button.

You can flag a square by right clicking (long press on mobile) on it to cycle through the pieces.
Flagging can be useful for keeping track of where pieces may be.

Board Information

After you guess specific squares, information about the board state is revealed through different coloured squares:

Black Squares - this square is empty and is not being targeted by any pieces

Red Squares - congratulations! You have revealed one of the pieces. It has ceased being active and targeting other squares.

Yellow Squares - the square is empty but is being targeted by the number of active pieces displayed on the square.
As pieces are revealed and 'taken off the board', the number of active pieces targeting a square may decrease.

Additional Information

If you fail to reveal all the pieces within 8 turns, 32 will be added to your score (number of guesses taken).
This means you have to weigh up getting a lower score by guessing less pieces per turn, and increasing your risk of not revealing all the piece in time and losing.
Note: pieces can target squares through other pieces, and can target each other (however information on the number of pieces targeting an occupied cell is not available)
